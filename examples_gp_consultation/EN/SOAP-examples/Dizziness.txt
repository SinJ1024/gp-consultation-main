Subjective:
Suffering from vertigo for 3 days. Occurs when getting out of bed. Symptoms increase with head movements. No hearing loss, tinnitus, speech problems, limb paralysis, fainting, recent illness, smoking, alcohol or drug use. However, nausea and palpitations occur with dizziness. Request for help: Wants to know what it is and get rid of the dizziness.


Objective:
Blood pressure: 110/70 mmHg, Pulse: 62 bpm.
Neurological examination: no paralysis, no nystagmus during tracking movement, positive Dix-Hallpike test on both sides.


Evaluation:
Benign paroxysmal positional vertigo


Plan:
Explanation of BPPV. Reassurance that it is not a brain tumour. Epley manoeuvre performed in the practice. Instructions for home via thuisarts.nl. Return if symptoms persist or worsen.
