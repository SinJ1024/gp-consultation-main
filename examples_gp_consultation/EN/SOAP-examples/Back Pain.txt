Subjective:
For a week and a half, pain in the back, localised on the right side. Pain radiates to the right leg, via the back of the thigh, side of the lower leg to the foot. Continuously present, no improvement with paracetamol and ibuprofen. Pain interferes with sleep and daily activities, including work and sports. No loss of strength, but pain interferes with movement. Dull feeling on the outside of the right lower leg, slight tingling. Normal feeling in riding breeches area, no problems with urination or defecation. Request for help: Wants to get rid of the pain.


Objective:
No visible abnormalities in the back or legs. No pain when pressure is applied to the vertebrae. Lifting the right leg causes pain, left leg without pain. Dull feeling on the right side of the leg and foot. Strength in both legs is equal. Reflexes are normal.


Evaluation:
Herniated disc


Plan:
Start pregabalin, build up slowly. Advice not to drive at the start and while building up the dosage. Referral to physiotherapist for exercises. Explanation of herniated disc and treatment. If symptoms worsen, such as loss of sensation in the riding breeches area, problems with urination/defecation, or loss of strength in the leg, contact immediately. Follow-up appointment in 1 week.
